Virgin Islands are a western island group of the Leeward Islands located in the Lesser Antilles on the border of the <a href='/continents/caribbean-population/' target='_blank'>Caribbean</a> Sea and the Atlantic Ocean. The eastern islands create the <a href='/countries/british-virgin-islands-population/' target='_blank'>British Virgin Islands</a> while the western islands make up the Virgin Islands of the <a href='/countries/united-states-population/' target='_blank'>United States</a>. The U.S. Virgin Islands consist of the main islands of Saint John, Saint Croix, Saint Thomas, the smaller Water Island, and surrounding small islands. The small country has a population density of 310 per square kilometer (804/sq mi), which ranks 42nd in the world. The capital and largest city is <a href='/us-cities/charlotte-population/' target='_blank'>Charlotte</a> Amalie on Saint Thomas with an estimated population of 19,000. The British Virgin Islands include Tortola (24,000), Anegada (200), Virgin Gorda (3,000) and Jost <a href='/world-cities/van-population/' target='_blank'>Van</a> Dyke (300). The largest city and capital is Road Town, with a population of around 9,000.</p>
<h2 id="virgin-islands-demographics">Virgin Islands Demographics</h2>
<p>76% of the population of the U.S. Virgin Islands are Afro-Caribbean (black), while 15.6% are white, 1.4% are Asian and 2.1% are mixed or some other ethnicity. Hispanic or Latino of any race account for 17.4% of the population (10.3% Puerto Rican, 5.4% Dominican). Most of the inhabitants today are descended from Africans who were enslaved and brought to the islands by Europeans to work on plantations.</p>
<p>The majority of the population on the British Virgin Island are Afro-Caribbean (83%) and are also descended from slaves. Europeans (mostly British) account for 7% of the population. The remaining 9.5% is comprised of Black/Carib mixed, Carib/Amerindian, Indian and mixed-race Hispanic people. About 4% of the population is Hispanic.</p>
